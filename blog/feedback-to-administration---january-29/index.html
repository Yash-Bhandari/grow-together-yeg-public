Protect our housing progress

We've come so far with our housing policy over the past two years. Don't let it all slip away.
Jacob Dawang

On February 10, City Council will consider torpedoing its own housing policy. Administration is proposing:

  • Reducing the number of homes allowed on standard interior lots from 8 to 6.
  • Increasing minimum lot size per home from 75 sqm to 90 sqm on interior lots.
  • Increasing minimum lot size per home from 75 sqm to 80 sqm on corner lots.

These reductions go much farther beyond any previous proposed reductions to unit counts and are a reactionary policy to opposition.

Administration will also present council with proposals on reducing height limits, but are not recommending them.

The deadline to provide feedback is January 29.

The Goal: We need you to tell them that Edmontonians want more housing, not less.

What to do:

Fill out City Administration's survey linked below.

Tell administration you support more housing choice

Tell them you oppose the following:

❌ Cutting unit counts: Keep the limit at 8 units (don't drop it to 6).

❌ Increasing site area: Keep it at 75m² (don't increase to 90m²).

❌ Reducing height: Maintain the 10.5m limit; don't add "height impact" restrictions.

Here's a short example paragraph you can use as a response. It's best if you can write your own response, but the template response is still appreciated and very helpful

"I oppose the proposal to reduce the maximum number of dwellings on interior sites from 8 to 6, and I oppose increasing the minimum site area per dwelling. The RS zone has improved neighbourhoods across the city by providing more housing options, reinvigorating local businesses, and bringing back life in places with declining population. Furthermore, I oppose any reduction in building height or new restrictions on third storeys. We should be making it easier to build diverse housing types, not harder."
